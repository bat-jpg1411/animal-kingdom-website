Ready to dive into a world of incredible creatures? Our updated Animal Kingdom is here to amaze and educate. Click the button below to see a collection of some of the most fascinating animals on the planet.

Omnivores

Omnivores are animals that eat both plants and other animals. They have a flexible diet that includes a mix of meat and vegetation.

Bear

Bears are omnivores that eat a wide variety of foods, including berries, insects, fish, and small mammals.

Bears have a fantastic sense of smell, seven times better than a bloodhound's.

Raccoon

The raccoon is a medium-sized mammal native to North America. Known for its "masked" face and dexterous front paws, it is a highly adaptable omnivore.

Raccoons are known to "wash" their food before eating, which is actually a way to soften and find small, hidden food items.

Pig

Pigs are domesticated, hoofed mammals that can eat both plants and animals.

Pigs are highly intelligent and can be trained to do tricks.

Fox

A fox is a small to medium-sized omnivorous mammal belonging to several genera of the family Canidae.

A fox's diet changes with the seasons, from insects in summer to small mammals in winter.

Carnivores

Carnivores are animals that primarily obtain food by eating other animals. Their diet is mostly or entirely meat.

Lion

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera, native to Africa and India. It is a powerful apex predator.

Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides.

Wolf

The wolf is a large canid native to Eurasia and North America. They are social animals that hunt in packs.

Wolves communicate with each other through howls, barks, growls, and whines.

Tiger

The tiger is the largest cat species, known for their distinctive striped coats.

A tiger's roar can be heard from up to 3 kilometers away.

Crocodile

Crocodiles are large, semi-aquatic reptiles that live throughout the tropics.

Crocodiles have the strongest bite force of any animal.

Herbivores

Herbivores are animals that eat only plants. Their digestive systems are specialized to break down vegetation.

Elephant

The elephant is the largest living land animal, and it eats a wide variety of plant material, including grasses, leaves, bark, and fruit.

An elephant's trunk contains about 40,000 muscles, making it extremely strong and flexible.

Cow

Cows are large, domesticated, bovine hoofed mammals. Their primary food source is grass and other plants.

Cows can sleep standing up but only dream when lying down.

Gorilla

Gorillas are ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous apes that inhabit the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa.

Gorillas are one of the most intelligent primates and can use tools.

Koala

The koala is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia.

Koalas have fingerprints that are almost identical to human fingerprints.
